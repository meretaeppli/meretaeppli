About

Welcome to my webpage!

I am a postdoctoral fellow in the department of Earth System Science at Stanford University. My goal is to understand the biological, chemical, and hydrological processes that control the turnover of carbon in soils and sediments. Understanding these processes is critical to advance our understanding of global carbon budgets and improve predictions of global carbon cycling in a changing climate. I am currently working with soils from the floodplains of East and Slate River in the Rocky Mountains, Colorado, and floodplains in the Lake Tahoe basin in the Sierra Nevada mountain range on the California/Nevada border. I use various spectroscopic, electrochemical and wet chemistry approaches to elucidate controls on carbon mineralization in these soils.

I was awarded the ETH Silver Medal for my doctoral thesis, which is awarded to the top 8% of doctoral candidates. I hold a Master's and Bachelor's degree in Environmental Science from the ETH. Prior to my time at ETH, I obtained a diploma in Music Performance and Production from London Music School. My research at Stanford is funded by a mobility fellowship from the Swiss National Science Foundation.
